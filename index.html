The problem

Three things broke AI safety hiring.
None of them are being fixed.

The field has job boards, fellowships, and Slack channels. What it does not have is a trusted signal. Every mechanism that was supposed to surface talent has a different failure mode.

01

Referrals dominate

The most reliable path into any AI safety role is a personal connection. Not qualifications, not portfolios. A recommendation from someone already inside. Talented people outside the network have no reliable pathway in, regardless of how relevant their skills are.

02

LLMs broke written applications

AI has made applying nearly free. Application volume no longer signals genuine interest. Cover letters no longer surface whether someone can think clearly. Orgs are drowning in high-quality-sounding but meaningless submissions with no consistent alternative in sight.

03

Five orgs, five first-round screens

Every AI safety org screens from scratch. The same candidate answers the same questions in five separate first-round calls. No portable signal. No shared rubric. No way for a candidate to build up a credential over time or understand what they are actually being evaluated on.

What pAIr is

One conversation.
A credential that travels.

The AI agent replaces the first-round screen with something candidates take once and carry everywhere. Orgs get a trusted score. Candidates get a portable profile.

01
A real conversation, not a form

A 30-45 minute AI agent interview that probes your background, reasoning about AI safety, and role-specific scenarios. Adaptive and hard to game — it follows up, challenges, and digs where a form cannot.

02
Scored against a field-built rubric

The rubric is built with input from senior people at MATS, ARC, Apollo, METR, and similar orgs. Not invented by a startup. That provenance is what makes the score trustworthy enough for orgs to act on.

03
A portable credential that travels with you

Score, rubric breakdown, and transcript summary — shareable with any org you apply to. Orgs get a trusted signal without scheduling a call. "pAIr score preferred" starts appearing in job descriptions.
